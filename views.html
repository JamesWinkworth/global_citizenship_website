Views on Abortion

Overview:

Abortion is a very divided topic. Many people believe that abortion is a mother's way of taking control of her body, and it gives her the freedom to do what she wants. Other people, however, believe in a pro-life view that life starts at conception, and therefore abortion is murder and should be banned, because only God is allowed to take life.

Reasons people choose to have an abortion may include:

  • To save the mother or child's life
  • Risk of severe disability
  • The mother isn't financially stable
  • Rape/sexual assault

    • Pro-Choice (For)

    • It's the woman's body, so she has the choice whether or not she wants a child.
    • The pregnant woman's health is more important than that of the embryo/foetus.
    • In the case of rape/assault, it would be unjust to deny a woman abortion.
    • The embryo/foetus does not have the same rights as the mother.
    • The quality of life of the mother or child could be negatively affected by the birth.
    • The woman may either be too young to bring the child up, or have commitments that make it too difficult.

    Pro-Life (Against)

    • Many people believe that life begins at conception, therefore abortion is morally wrong because it takes life away.
    • The unborn child does not have a choice.
    • There are alternatives to abortion e.g. adoption or legal guardianship, as well as ways to prevent it e.g. contraception.
    • Abortion destroys human life, making life seem cheap and expendable.
    • People born with disabilities can still live happy lives.
    • Everyone, including an embryo or foetus, has a right to live, which abortion takes away.

    Religious Views:

    Christianity:

    On the whole, Christians oppose abortion, most notably Roman Catholics. They believe in the 'sanctity of life', which states that all life is a gift from God and is sacred. However, some Christians may accept abortion under certain circumstances, e.g. if the mother's life is at risk. Regarding abortion, the General Synod stated: The Church of England combines strong opposition to abortion with a recognition that there can be - strictly limited - conditions under which it may be morally preferable to any available alternative.

    Islam:

    Similar to Christians, Muslims believe in the 'sancity of life', and that all life is sacred and should be protected. The Qur'an states: Whosoever has spared the life of a soul, it is as though he has spared the life of all people. Whosoever has killed a soul, it is as though he has murdered all of mankind. However, many Muslims may accept circumstances for abortion because it may be seen as the 'lesser of two evils', meaning the least evil compared to alternatives.

    Buddhism:

    Traditional Buddhism is very opposed to abortion, as it meets the 5 conditions required to constitute the act of killing. However, as with other religions, more modern buddhists think it depends on the situation, with the Dalai Lama stating: Of course, abortion, from a Buddhist viewpoint, is an act of killing ... but it depends on the circumstances. If the unborn child will be retarded or if the birth will create serious problems for the parent, these are cases where there can be an exception. I think abortion should be approved or disapproved according to each circumstance.

    Judaism:

    Strict Judaism permits abortion only when it poses a threat to the mother's life. This includes if she is at risk of death, or if the mother may be in distress, as well as if she was a victim of rape/incest. Other than that, abortion is not permitted, as Judaism has a very strict view about the sancity of life. According to the Mishnah: Whoever destroys one life is as if he destroyed a whole world, and whoever preserves a life is as if he preserved the whole world.

    Hinduism:

    Hindu views stem from the idea of 'ahimsa', meaning non-violence. This means that Hindus are generally very opposed to abortion, unless it is to save the life of the mother. One Hindu text compared abortion to the killing of a priest, and another one said that abortion is an even worse sin than killing the parents.
